TL;DR — the short answer

For a startup, every signup is expensive and most go silent. ConversionCRM tracks behavior with one script tag, scores every user 0–100, stages them, and sends 8 automated emails — welcome to win-back — under your name. Free during beta, no credit card, founder pricing locked at launch.

Q: What is ConversionCRM?

A: ConversionCRM is free-trial conversion software for SaaS. You add one script tag, and it tracks each user's behavior, scores their engagement 0–100 across six layers, assigns a lifecycle stage, and automatically sends behavior-triggered emails that move free users to paid — installed in about three minutes, Free plan included.

The founder problem

You're the growth team. And the support team. And…

01

Signups outpace attention

Launch days bring 80 signups; you personally email 6 of them. The other 74 decide your product's fate in silence. Convert signups to paying users only works as a system, not a heroic effort.

02

Tools assume a team

Journey builders, segment logic, MAU pricing — most customer activation software is built for someone whose whole job this is. Yours is shipping product.

03

Timing beats copy

The perfect upgrade email sent a week late converts nobody. Without buying-intent detection, you're always a week late.

Playbook

From launch post to paying users

01

Install before you launch

Add the widget the day before your Product Hunt / HN / X launch so every single signup is tracked and scored from minute one.

02

Set the aha event you'd bet on

The one action that separates keepers from tourists. Don't overthink — you can change it in Settings as you learn.

03

Let welcome + nudges run

Welcome fires instantly on each signup. Feature nudges go to anyone who stalls in Onboarding. You do nothing.

04

Personally email the hot list

Each morning, open the dashboard, sort by score, and send personal notes to Conversion-ready users via the composer. Ten minutes, highest-leverage sales you'll do all day.

05

Watch win-backs resurrect launches

Two weeks later, the win-back email quietly recovers users from your launch spike you'd have otherwise lost forever.
