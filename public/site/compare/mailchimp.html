ConversionCRM vs Mailchimp

Mailchimp is built for broadcast email — newsletters, campaigns, and audience marketing. ConversionCRM is built for SaaS product lifecycle email — scoring what users do inside your app and sending the right email the moment they're ready to convert. Different inputs, different outputs.

Start free See how it works
TL;DR — the short answer

Choose ConversionCRM if your goal is converting free-trial or freemium users to paid using their actual in-app behavior as the trigger. Choose Mailchimp if you are running a newsletter, a broadcast campaign, or audience-based email marketing for a non-SaaS audience. For product-led SaaS teams, these tools serve different layers and are most often used together rather than instead of each other.

Q: What is ConversionCRM?

A: ConversionCRM is free-trial conversion software for SaaS. You add one script tag, and it tracks each user's behavior, scores their engagement 0–100 across six layers, assigns a lifecycle stage, and automatically sends behavior-triggered emails that move free users to paid — installed in about three minutes, Free plan included.

At a glance

Email marketing for audiences vs email automation for product users

Mailchimp knows who opened your last campaign and whether they clicked a link. ConversionCRM knows which feature a user explored yesterday, how many sessions they've had, whether they visited the pricing page, and what their engagement score is today. That difference determines which emails get sent — and whether they convert.

The real difference

Mailchimp knows who opened your email.
ConversionCRM knows what they did in your product.

Mailchimp's automation triggers are contact-list-centric: a tag is applied, an audience membership changes, or a date arrives. ConversionCRM's triggers are behavior-centric: a user visits the pricing page three times, their engagement score crosses a threshold, or they exhaust a feature limit. The first model works for audience email. The second model is what SaaS products actually need to convert trials — see the full walkthrough.

Where ConversionCRM wins

  • Tracks actual in-app behavior (page views, feature use, session depth) — not email-open history
  • 6-layer engagement scoring recomputed nightly: recency, frequency, depth, key feature, time spent, buying intent
  • 8 prebuilt lifecycle emails wired to stages — welcome, onboarding nudge, mid-trial check-in, upgrade offer, urgency, win-back — no audience segmentation required
  • Detects upgrade intent from pricing visits and quota exhaustion; stages users as Conversion Ready automatically
  • Three-minute install with no list import — tracks identified product users from day one

Where Mailchimp wins

  • Newsletter and broadcast campaigns to your entire audience — announcements, product updates, content distribution
  • Ecommerce integrations: Shopify, WooCommerce, abandoned cart recovery
  • Landing pages, signup forms, and lead capture built into the same tool
  • Audience segmentation by demographics, email engagement, and custom tags
  • Large ecosystem of integrations for non-SaaS and marketing-focused teams
Honest take

Which one should you pick?

Pick ConversionCRM if…

  • Your goal is free trial to paid conversion or freemium upgrade rate
  • You want emails that react to what users do in the product, not to list tags
  • You are a founder or small SaaS team without a dedicated email marketing hire
  • You need engagement scoring and lifecycle stages without building an event pipeline

Pick Mailchimp if…

  • You run newsletters or broadcast campaigns to a marketing audience
  • Your email needs center on ecommerce — abandoned carts, purchase follow-ups
  • You need landing pages and forms to build your subscriber list
  • You are not building SaaS — Mailchimp's broadcast model fits non-product companies well
